# Gender Order

## Legal & ritual basis

Female dominance rests on physiology recast as cosmology: women bear the sacred
经血, carry mandate, and are physically the stronger sex after long social
selection (height, bone density, strength, endurance). Men are held to be
"血薄体柔" — thin-blooded, delicate, suited to the inner sphere.

## Ideology: "different spheres"

The state never preaches male inferiority. It preaches division:

> 女子主外，是承经血、奉天命、护社稷。
> 男子守内，是养血脉、奉宗嗣、全其柔贵。

Inequality is packaged as natural order and protection.

## Male dependency

Men gain value through:
- **Marriage / consortship** (后宫 or household spouse).
- **Service** (侍奉) — manners, music, calligraphy, household management.
- **承养 (blood-nursing)** — sustaining a 胎息 with one's own blood; a scarce,
  non-transferable, politically valuable qualification (see
  [`30-bloodline-pregnancy.md`](30-bloodline-pregnancy.md)).

## Why men do not govern

Men may study **内学** (etiquette, blood-nursing canon, household accounts) but are
barred from **外学** and office: no holding court rank, command, ritual primacy,
law, or governance. The stated law:

> 女子主外，承天经、理军国、祭社稷、掌刑名。
> 男子主内，守血脉、奉宗嗣、理内宅、养胎息。
> 男子不得应外试，不得受外官，不得私习兵律政书。

A man secretly studying 兵法/律令 is a recurring source of dramatic conflict.
